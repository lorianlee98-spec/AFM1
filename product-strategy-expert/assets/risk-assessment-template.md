## 2. Risk Identification

### 2.1 Risk Categories

#### Technical Risks
- **Definition**: Risks related to technology, infrastructure, and development
- **Examples**: Technical debt, scalability issues, integration challenges, security vulnerabilities

#### Market Risks
- **Definition**: Risks related to market acceptance, competition, and market conditions
- **Examples**: Low market demand, intense competition, changing market trends, regulatory changes

#### Operational Risks
- **Definition**: Risks related to operations, processes, and resources
- **Examples**: Resource shortages, process inefficiencies, communication breakdowns, vendor issues

#### Financial Risks
- **Definition**: Risks related to budget, costs, and financial viability
- **Examples**: Cost overruns, funding shortages, revenue shortfalls, financial market volatility

#### Strategic Risks
- **Definition**: Risks related to strategic decisions and alignment
- **Examples**: Misalignment with business goals, poor strategic decisions, missed opportunities, changing business priorities

#### Legal and Compliance Risks
- **Definition**: Risks related to legal requirements and compliance
- **Examples**: Regulatory non-compliance, legal disputes, intellectual property issues, data privacy violations

## 4. Risk Mitigation Strategies

### 4.1 Mitigation Approaches

#### Avoidance
- **Definition**: Eliminating the risk by changing the project plan or approach
- **When to use**: When the risk is severe and avoidance is feasible
- **Example**: Avoiding a high-risk technology by selecting a more mature alternative

#### Reduction
- **Definition**: Reducing the probability or impact of the risk
- **When to use**: When the risk cannot be avoided but can be managed
- **Example**: Conducting additional testing to reduce the probability of defects

#### Transfer
- **Definition**: Transferring the risk to another party
- **When to use**: When another party can better manage the risk
- **Example**: Purchasing insurance to transfer financial risk

#### Acceptance
- **Definition**: Accepting the risk and preparing for its potential impact
- **When to use**: When the risk is low or mitigation is not cost-effective
- **Example**: Accepting a small risk of schedule delay and building buffer time
